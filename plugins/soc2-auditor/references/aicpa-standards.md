# AICPA Professional Standards Reference

Applicable standards for SOC 2 examinations.

---

## AT-C Section 205 — Examination Engagements

AT-C 205 is the primary standard governing SOC 2 examinations.

### Key Requirements

#### Preconditions (AT-C 205.10–.14)

1. The responsible party (management) must:
   - Accept responsibility for the subject matter
   - Accept responsibility for selecting the criteria (Trust Service Criteria)
   - Accept responsibility for determining that criteria are suitable
   - Provide the practitioner with a written assertion
   - Provide access to all relevant information

2. The practitioner must:
   - Be independent
   - Have competence in the subject matter
   - Assess whether the criteria are suitable and available to intended users

#### Planning (AT-C 205.15–.22)

1. Obtain understanding of the subject matter and criteria
2. Assess risks of material misstatement
3. Design procedures responsive to assessed risks
4. Consider materiality in planning and performing the examination
5. Consider the effect of information technology on the subject matter

#### Evidence (AT-C 205.23–.35)

1. Obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to reduce examination risk to an acceptably low level
2. Types of evidence:
   - **Inspection** — examining records, documents, or tangible assets
   - **Observation** — witnessing a process or procedure being performed
   - **Inquiry** — seeking information from knowledgeable persons
   - **Confirmation** — obtaining representations from third parties
   - **Recalculation** — checking mathematical accuracy
   - **Reperformance** — independently executing procedures or controls
   - **Analytical procedures** — evaluating information through analysis of relationships
3. Evidence from external sources is generally more reliable than internal sources
4. Written evidence is more reliable than oral representations

#### Using the Work of Others (AT-C 205.36–.42)

1. May use the work of internal auditors or other practitioners
2. Must evaluate competence, objectivity, and the nature/scope of work performed
3. Must perform sufficient procedures to determine whether work is adequate

#### Written Representations (AT-C 205.43–.48)

1. Obtain written representations from the responsible party (management)
2. Representations must be dated as of the date of the practitioner's report
3. Required representations include:
   - Acknowledgment of responsibility for the subject matter
   - Statement that the description is fairly presented
   - Statement that controls are suitably designed (Type I) and operating effectively (Type II)
   - Disclosure of known fraud or noncompliance

#### Forming the Opinion (AT-C 205.49–.56)

1. **Unqualified opinion** — subject matter is in accordance with criteria in all material respects
2. **Qualified opinion** — except for specific matters, subject matter conforms
3. **Adverse opinion** — subject matter is NOT in accordance with criteria in a material respect
4. **Disclaimer** — unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence

## SSAE 18 (AT-C Section 320) — Reporting on an Examination of Controls

### SOC 2 Report Structure

#### Type I Report — Design Effectiveness at a Point in Time

1. **Section I** — Independent Service Auditor's Report (Opinion)
2. **Section II** — Management's Assertion
3. **Section III** — Description of the Service Organization's System
4. **Section IV** — Service Auditor's Description of Tests and Results
   - Tests of design effectiveness only
   - No testing of operating effectiveness

#### Type II Report — Operating Effectiveness Over a Period

1. **Section I** — Independent Service Auditor's Report (Opinion)
2. **Section II** — Management's Assertion
3. **Section III** — Description of the Service Organization's System
4. **Section IV** — Service Auditor's Description of Tests of Controls and Results
   - Tests of both design AND operating effectiveness
   - Includes nature, timing, extent of tests
   - Reports results including exceptions

### Report Requirements

#### Management's Assertion Must State:

1. The description is presented in accordance with the description criteria
2. Controls were suitably designed to provide reasonable assurance (Type I and Type II)
3. Controls operated effectively throughout the specified period (Type II only)

#### Service Auditor's Report Must Include:

1. Title: "Independent Service Auditor's Report"
2. Addressee
3. Identification of the description, management's assertion, and the criteria
4. Statement of the service auditor's responsibility
5. Summary of procedures performed
6. Opinion on whether:
   - Description is fairly presented (in all material respects)
   - Controls are suitably designed (Type I and Type II)
   - Controls operated effectively (Type II only)
7. Statement that the examination was conducted in accordance with attestation standards
8. Inherent limitations paragraph
9. Restriction on use (intended users)
10. Service auditor's signature and date

## Quality Control Standards

### SQCS No. 8 / QM Section 10

1. **Engagement acceptance and continuance** — Assess risk, competence, independence before accepting
2. **Leadership responsibilities** — Partner sets tone, assigns appropriate staff
3. **Human resources** — Staff must have competence for the engagement
4. **Engagement performance** — Supervision, review, consultation
5. **Monitoring** — Firm-level quality monitoring applies

### Engagement Quality Review (EQR)

Required for high-risk engagements. The EQR reviewer must:

1. Be independent of the engagement team
2. Review key judgments made by the engagement team
3. Evaluate the significant risks identified
4. Review the engagement team's response to those risks
5. Review the report and consider whether it is appropriate

## Independence Requirements

### ET Section 1.200 — Independence

1. No financial interest in the client
2. No close business relationships
3. No employment/association relationships
4. Must be maintained throughout the engagement period
5. Threats to independence: self-review, advocacy, familiarity, undue influence, financial interest

### Non-Attest Services

The service auditor may NOT:
1. Make management decisions
2. Perform management functions
3. Accept responsibility for internal controls
4. Design or implement controls being examined

## Documentation Requirements

### AT-C 205.A79–.A81

Documentation must be sufficient to enable an experienced practitioner with no previous connection to the engagement to understand:

1. The nature, timing, and extent of procedures performed
2. The evidence obtained and its source
3. The conclusions reached

### Retention

- Engagement documentation should be assembled within 60 days of the report release date
- Retention period: minimum 5 years from report date (or longer per firm policy)

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Subject matter** | The service organization's system and controls |
| **Criteria** | Trust Service Criteria (AICPA 2017/2022) |
| **Responsible party** | Management of the service organization |
| **Practitioner** | The service auditor (CPA firm) |
| **Material misstatement** | A deficiency significant enough to influence user decisions |
| **Exception** | A control deviation from expected operation — may or may not be material |
| **Deficiency** | A control that is not suitably designed or not operating effectively |
